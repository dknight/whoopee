# NES Toolbox

A curated list of useful links for NES development. I update this list regularly.

_Last update: <time>2026-04-23</time>_

## Emulators

- [Mesen](https://www.mesen.ca/docs/index.html) — one of the best NES/Famicom emulators; includes a debugger.
- [FCEUX](https://fceux.com/web/home.html) — my personal favorite; runs well on Linux and includes a debugger.
- [iNES](http://fms.komkon.org/iNES/#Downloads) — a solid cross-platform emulator.

## Compilers and assembly

- [ca65](https://cc65.github.io/doc/ca65.html) — the 6502 assembler from the cc65 suite; supports targets such as the NES, C64, and Apple II.
- [Online 6502 Assembly Simulator](https://tony-cruise.github.io/6502Simulator.html) — quick in-browser testing (by Tony Cruise).

## Code editors and IDE

- My [nes.nvim](https://github.com/dknight/nes.nvim) Neovim plugin for NES development.
- [Visual Studio Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/download) with 6502 extensions.

## Graphics and CHR editors

- [NEXTT](https://frankengraphics.itch.io/nexxt) — a great CHR and sprite editor for NES.
- [YY-CHR](https://www.smwcentral.net/?p=section&a=details&id=27208) — a solid ROM-hacking and sprite editor.

## Sound editors

- [FamiTracker](http://famitracker.com/) — a simple, popular tracker for NES music.
- [FamiStudio](https://famistudio.org/) — a more advanced music composition tool.

## Books

 - "[Classic Game Programming on the NES: Make your own retro video game](https://www.amazon.com/Classic-Game-Programming-NES-retro/dp/1633438015)" by Tony Cruise.
 - "Programming the 6502" by Rodnay Zaks, ISBN 0895881357
